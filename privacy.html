Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2025 — and honestly, probably never again.

Who is collecting your data?

Me. Just me. One person, a laptop, too many side projects, and a deep conviction that I need a robot to text me when I've forgotten someone's birthday.

What data is collected?

The only phone number in this system is mine. The only person receiving texts is me. The only person who could possibly be surprised by any of this is also me, at 7am, when I've forgotten I built something the night before.

How is it used?

SMS messages are sent for personal automation purposes — things like birthday reminders, restaurant suggestions, and other small agents I've built for fun. No marketing. No third-party sharing. No profit motive. Just vibes and webhooks.

Is your data shared with anyone?

Absolutely not. There is no "your data." There is only my data. I am the user, the developer, the data subject, and the entire target demographic of this operation.

Cookies?

This is a static HTML page with no JavaScript, no tracking, and no cookies. It exists solely to satisfy a regulatory checkbox so Twilio will let me text myself. You're welcome, compliance.

How can you contact us?

You can contact me by... texting me, I suppose, though I should warn you that my phone number is closely guarded by the very agent you're reading about.

Changes to this policy

If I ever build something that involves other people's data, I'll write a real privacy policy. Until then, this one stands. It is, in all meaningful ways, complete.

This privacy policy is for a personal project involving automated SMS messages sent to the developer's own phone number. No user data is collected, stored, or processed beyond what is necessary to deliver a text message to myself.
